Martin Luther King Jr.

This man became the most visible spokesman and leader in the civil rights movement from 1955 until his assassination in 1968.

A timeline of Mr Luther King's life.

  • 1929 - Born as Michael King Jr. january 15th in Atlanta, Georgia, U.S.
  • 1944 - Gave his first public speech where he stated "black America still wears chains. The finest negro is at the mercy of the meanest white man. Even winners of our highest honors face the class color bar".
  • 1948 - Graduated from Morehouse highschool with a Bachelor of Arts in sociology aged nineteen.
  • 1955 - King received a Ph.D. degree.
  • 1955 - King's role in the bus boycott transformed him into a national figure and best-known spokesman of the civil rights movement.
  • 1957 - Fondation of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference to conduct nonviolent protests in the service of civil rights reform.
  • 1963 - King delivered a 17 minutes speech later known as "I have a Dream".
  • 1964 - Won the Nobel Peace Prize for combating racial inequality through nonviolent resistance.
  • 1968 - April 4th was fatally shot at the Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tenessee.
